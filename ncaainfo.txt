## 🏀 What Is March Madness (and a Bracket)?

**March Madness** is the annual NCAA men’s basketball tournament where **68 college teams** compete in a single-elimination showdown.

A **bracket** is simply a prediction chart where you:

* Pick who wins each game
* Advance winners round by round
* End with one national champion

Once a team loses, they’re out — no second chances.

---

## 🧾 How Filling a Bracket Works (Quick Walkthrough)

1. **Round of 64**
   You choose winners for each matchup.

2. **Round of 32 → Sweet 16 → Elite 8**
   Keep picking winners from your previous picks.

3. **Final Four → Championship**
   One final pick crowns the champion.

Points are usually awarded for each correct pick, with later rounds worth more.

## 🤠 Cowboy Version (Short & Fun)

Think of it like a rodeo:

* 68 bulls enter the arena

* One bull walks away with the belt buckle 🏆

## 🔁 Can You Prefill a March Madness Bracket?

Yes — **absolutely**, and many people do.

### Common prefilling strategies

* **All favorites** (higher seeds always win)
* **Seed-based logic** (e.g., 1-seeds go far, 12-seed upsets)
* **Randomized bracket** (pure chaos, surprisingly effective)
* **Stats-based** (KenPom, efficiency ratings, Vegas odds)

Most bracket platforms (ESPN, CBS, Yahoo) allow:

* Auto-pick favorites
* Random fill
* Manual override afterward

---

## 🧠 Can You Programmatically Prefill One?

Yes — if you’re building your own tool or app.

To do this, you need **two things**:

1. The **list of teams and matchups**
2. A **rule or model** to choose winners

---

## 📋 How to Fetch Teams & Matchups (Important Part)

There are **three realistic ways** to get the options list.

---

### ✅ Option 1: Public / Semi-Public APIs (Best for Devs)

Some commonly used data sources:

* **SportsDataIO (NCAA Basketball API)**
* **API-Sports (NCAA Basketball)**
* **RapidAPI NCAA endpoints**

They provide:

* Tournament bracket structure
* Team names
* Seeds
* Game IDs
* Results (as games progress)

⚠️ Most require:

* Free tier or paid key
* Attribution

---

### ✅ Option 2: Scraping Bracket Sites (Technically Possible)

You *can* scrape:

* ESPN
* NCAA.com
* CBS Sports

But:

* HTML changes often
* Legal/ToS considerations apply
* Not recommended for production apps

Good only for **personal experiments**.

---

### ✅ Option 3: Static Bracket Data (Simplest)

Many developers:

* Download the official bracket once
* Store it as JSON or CSV
* Prefill picks locally

Example structure:

```json
{
  "round": 1,
  "game_id": 1,
  "team_a": "Duke",
  "team_b": "Vermont",
  "seed_a": 2,
  "seed_b": 15
}
```

From there, your logic fills winners.

---

## 🧠 Example Prefill Logic (Simple)

* If seed difference ≥ 5 → pick lower seed
* Otherwise → coin flip
* Force at least one upset per region
* Always send one underdog to Sweet 16

This makes your bracket:

* Non-boring
* Surprisingly competitive

---

## 🧪 Can This Be Automated End-to-End?

Yes. A full flow looks like:

```text
Fetch teams & matchups
        ↓
Apply pick logic / model
        ↓
Generate bracket structure
        ↓
Export to UI / PDF / submission form
```

Some people even:

* Generate **1000 brackets**
* Submit the statistically strongest one

---

## ⚠️ One Honest Reality Check

March Madness is famously unpredictable.

Even the **best models** rarely score above:

* ~70% accuracy early rounds
* ~20–30% for final champion

That’s why it’s fun — and cruel.
